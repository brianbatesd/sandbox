On May 1, Vancouver Mayor Anne McEnerny-Ogle proclaimed the month of May “Small Business Month” in Vancouver. 

Nationally, more than half of Americans either own or work for a small business, and they create two out of three new jobs in the U.S. each year. Small businesses are the backbone of our economy. Locally, 95 percent of our businesses are small businesses with 50 or fewer employees. They provide jobs, services and amenities to our community.  They are the heart of Vancouver’s economy.  

During Vancouver's Small Business Month, the City takes this opportunity to highlight the impact of outstanding entrepreneurs and small business owners, and recognizes that Small Business Month is a time for us to celebrate the successes of small business.  During May, we'd like to thank our small business owners and employees for their contributions to our City, our economy, and our nation.

Throughout the month, our numerous partner agencies will offer online workshops and programs to assist small businesses, which are featured on the City’s Small Business Month Calendar.  On this calendar, you'll also find fun, community events and other opportunities for you to support your local business neighbors.

Connect and Navigate Small Business Resource Fair

On May 11, the City is partnering with 13 Small Business Providers and the Fort Vancouver Regional Library to host a free Small Business Resource Fair from 3 to 6 p.m. in the Community Room at the Downtown Vancouver library.  Startups and growing businesses can drop in to learn about business assistance agencies that provide free classes, technical training, small business loans and other helpful programs - all in one location. 

Read About Our Ongoing Programs

Small Business Month is just one component of Vancouver’s commitment to a thriving business community. The City offers year-round assistance through these business-friendly programs:

Procurement Program

The City has some great services that are tailor-made for small or growing businesses.  For example, most small business owners are not aware that they can bid on smaller projects, jobs, or contracts with the City of Vancouver.  Doing business with the City’s Procurement Program helps small businesses grow their project pipeline, adding to their bottom line.  Hiring local recirculates money spent here 2-4 times more than money spent with a non-local company.  Hiring local is not only smart and economical, more importantly, it's the right thing to do, and helps to ensure that equity is embedded in our city’s infrastructure projects.

Pre-Lease Program

The City of Vancouver's Pre-Lease Program is for business owners who want to lease, buy or renovate a commercial space.  Staff from city departments - Health Department, Fire Marshal's Office, Permitting, Sewer, Land Use Planning, Transportation, Engineering, Building, and others - all meet with the owner and architect in the proposed space for a building walk-through before they invest or sign any contracts for the space. City staff provide expertise, comments, and answer questions. This one-hour meeting offers the business or property owner a full spectrum in understanding what changes and improvements are needed before they invest or sign legal documents, saving them substantial research, meeting time, and potentially unknown investment costs. Owners learn:  business type allowed in space based on zoning, current occupancy classification, permitting process for proposed changes, and much more - all in a single site visit in under one hour. 

Business Assistance and Funding

One of the biggest challenges we see with small businesses is access to capital, and having limited bandwidth and limited time to identify or pursue business and technical resources.  To help, City staff will shop for federal, local and state grants each week, adding them to the City's Business & Worker Assistance public webpage. In addition to this, the City’s small business provider partners will offer grants or funding specials, which are also included on the webpage, saving business owners time and research, and providing them with a one-stop-shop to find funding or capital assistance opportunities.
