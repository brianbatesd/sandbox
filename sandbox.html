Government program helps with broadband service and internet devices: The Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) is a U.S. government program run by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) program to help low-income households pay for internet service and for connected devices, like a laptop or tablet.

You may be eligible if your household’s income is below 200% of the Federal Poverty Line, or if you or someone you live with receives a government benefit like SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, WIC, Pell Grant, or Free and Reduced-Price Lunch. If your household is eligible, you could receive:

  • Up to a $30/month discount on your internet service
  • Up to a $75/month discount if your household is on qualifying Tribal lands
  • A one-time discount of up to $100 for a laptop, tablet, or desktop computer (with a co-payment of more than $10 but less than $50)

Learn more here. To receive the connected device discount, consumers need to enroll in the ACP with a participating provider that offers connected devices (Note: not all internet company offer device discounts). The internet company will provide the discount to the applicant.

Business License Surcharge (BLS) Fee Refunds Extended for Eligible Businesses: Local businesses most impacted by continued pandemic-related restrictions will continue to benefit from a program to refund the City’s business license surcharge (BLS) fee, an “employee fee” that is calculated per full time employee. The Vancouver City Council recently approved extending the refund program through the end of 2022.

The program, which launched April 1, 2021 and now will continue through December 2022, is an extension of the city’s temporary business license fee waiver emergency order approved in April 2020 to provide immediate economic relief to all city business license holders impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic.

The newly extended refund program is targeted toward businesses that have traditionally served large numbers of customers indoors, simultaneously. These businesses are among those most severely impacted by the pandemic, are likely to remain subject to some of the longest restrictions, and are particularly well-situated to utilize BLS fee savings to support increased consumer safety. Specifically, the businesses include:

  • Restaurants, taverns, breweries, wineries and distilleries
  • Fitness and training facilities
  • Movie theaters, theaters, and performing arts venues
  • Bowling facilities.

Eligible businesses seeking refunds will be required to first continue with their usual license registration and fee payment process through the Washington State Department of Revenue. Then, those businesses will need to complete a City of Vancouver business license surcharge refund application form to receive the refund. New businesses will need to first apply for a city business license.

A link to the refund application is available at www.cityofvancouver.us/CovidSurchargeRefund. If your business meets the eligibility requirements listed, you can request a refund by completing the application form and returning it to the City no later than January 31, 2023.

Business owners who have questions about the program may call city licensing staff at 360-487-8410, option 3, or send an email to business.licenses@cityofvancouver.us.

2021 PPP Program Update: The U.S. Small Business Administration closed the Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) as of May 31, 2021. During PPP's 2021 application period, eligible businesses in Washington state have received almost $5.9 B in loans from the program, placing it as the 12th most nationwide by state in dollars.

Focus has now turned to the loan forgiveness process. Steps for loan recipients to apply for loan forgiveness include:

  1. Contact your PPP lender and complete the correct form or online portal.
  2. Compile documentation.
  3. Submit the forgiveness form and documentation if required to your lender.
  4. Continue to communicate with your lender through the process.
  5. All loan forgiveness forms can be submitted up to 10 months after the end of the borrower’s covered period. Loan forgiveness applications can be submitted when the borrower:
    • Has used all of the funds.
    • Documented use of the loan proceeds is compiled.
    • The lender is ready to accept the application(s).

More information on loan forgiveness can be found here or by contacting your lender directly.

June 5, 2020

PPP Program Changes: The Paycheck Protection Program Flexibility Act (PPPFA) has been approved by Congress and signed into law by the President. PPPFA addresses many concerns expressed by the small business community around the Paycheck Protection Program (PPP), which provides COVID-19 relief to small businesses. Learn more here.

Among others, PPP changes include:

  • Reduces the amount of the loan needed to be spent on payroll from 75% to 60%, thus increasing the amount of funds available for other expenses from 25% to 40%.
  • Extends time period to use funds from 8 to 24 weeks.
  • Pushes back a June 30 deadline to rehire workers to December 31, 2020.
