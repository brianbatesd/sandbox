Determining employee count for employee fee calculation

For a new business license, report estimated total hours to be worked in city in coming 12 months by all employees; and for renewing license holders, report total hours worked in city in previous 12 months or 4 quarters by all employees. (See definition of “employees” below.) Total hours are then divided by 1,664 hours, and result is rounded to nearest whole number to determine employee count for the purpose of employee fee calculation; see examples below. The minimum employee count is one.

Employee count calculation examples. Example 1: 600 hours divided by 1,664 hours equals 0.36, rounded to 1. Example 2: 6,000 hours divided by 1,664 hours equals 3.6, rounded to 4. Example 3: 75,000 hours divided by 1,664 hours equals 45.1, rounded to 45.

“Employee” definition: Pay-rolled employees, self-employed persons, sole proprietors, owners, managers, and partners that work in city limits.
