A pilot program to enhance and restore green spaces in the City of Vancouver

About the Program

The Naturespaces Program is a pilot program bringing the community together to participate in restoration and enhancement projects at select sites in the park system throughout the City of Vancouver.

Volunteers can experience impactful, satisfying and fun hands-on activities that support the natural environment in the park system and other public sites, such as:

  • identifying and inventorying native and invasive plants
  • pruning native plants and trees
  • amending soils with compost and mulch
  • removing invasive English ivy and other unwanted plants
  • planting or rehoming new native and beneficial plants
  • creating habitat features for pollinators

Naturespaces will also offer educational opportunities like nature walks, workshops on Pacific Northwest plants, animals, geology, northwest gardening and habitat design, as well as a Stewardship Training Program, empowering residents with current knowledge of Vancouver’s natural history and the City’s committed actions to expand and enhance our green spaces for improved pollinator and wildlife conservation, expanded urban forest tree canopy cover, and clean air and water.
